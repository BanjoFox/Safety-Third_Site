## General Terms

---

**Aftercare**
: The timespan after a scene which participants in the scene use to recover from the activity.

    ---

**Boot Black**
: Someone that offers leather care services to the community. While this is often a free service it is very common to offer monerary tipsas at a fair rate of exchange.

    ---

**Consent**
: Agreement between parties regarding a negotiated set of boundaries.

    ---

**Dungeon**
: A physical space where individuals are able to partake in kinky activities

    **NOTE**: The term "Dungeon" is synonymous with the term "Playspace", and both are used in reference to the same thing.  There may also be instances where either term are used for the entire location, and the other is used for the specific areas within that location where play is allowed.

    ---

**(Modern) Kink**
: A broad range of activities in which one or more participants engage in activities that are typically considered “non-normative”.

    ---

**Munch**
: A gathering at a _public space_, often a bar/restaurant, that allows individuals to participate in casual conversation, and meet others.

    ---

**Negotiation**
: Mutual discussion about the terms of a particular scene in which the participants establish rules, boundaries, and share expectations.

    ---

**Play**
: The act of partaking in a kinky activity, regardless of role

    ---

**Playspace** - _see also_ [Dungeon](02-Dictionary-of-Kink "Dungeon")
: Synonymous with the term Dungeon as described above

    ---

**Role(s)**
: A loosely-defined part taken on by individuals in particular social-settings.

    ---

**Scene**
: The period of time during which one or more participants engage in a kinky activity.

    ---

**Service (Role)** or **Service-Type**
: Any individual that takes on a role (top/bottom, dom/sub, etc.) for the benefit of another.

    **Note:** Motivations for being a Service-Type varies between individuals, although general consensus is that they find enjoyment in being able to provide the service, and not directly from the act itself.

    ---

## Kink Relationship Dynamics

---

**Power Exchange**
: Negotiated power imbalance, sometimes referred to as Authority Transfer.

    ---

**Top**  
: An individual that gives sensation during kinky play.

    ---

**Bottom**  
: An individual that receives sensation during kinky play.

    ---

**Dominant**  
: An individual that receives control and/or authority over another individual.

    ---

**Submissive**  
: An individual that gives control and/or authority over to another individual

    ---

## Safety Acronyms

---

**SCC**
: Safe, Sane, Consensual

    **Note:** Currently considered to be deprecated, even by its originator (David Stein), because the phrase has become overly diluted. Some folks would even pose the joke: "Safe, Sane, Consensual; pick two." as a way of hinting that kink-play always carries some form of risk, and cannot (generally), embody all three statements simultaneously. It is also important to note that consent is considered to be a hard requirement at all times.

    ---

**RACK**
: Risk Aware Consensual Kink (Coined by Gary Switch, 1999\)

    **Note:** Currently considered to be the most appropriate acronym for kink safety as it reminds participants that they need to be aware of the risks for the activities they choose to participate in.

    ---

**PRICK**
: Personal Responsibility Informed Kink

    **Note**: This acronym puts more emphasis on the concept that kink participants are ultimately responsible for their own safety.
